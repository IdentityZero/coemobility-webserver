COEMobility Team

At the College of Engineering, a groundbreaking project is underway, spearheaded by a talented group of 4th-year students from the BS in Computer Engineering program, Section 4B. Leveraging the power of RFID technology and the versatility of Raspberry Pi, they are developing a Vehicle Identification and Parking Space Monitoring system.

This innovative system aims to revolutionize parking management within the college campus. By affixing RFID tags to vehicles, each vehicle becomes uniquely identifiable within the system. As vehicles enter the designated parking areas, RFID readers installed at strategic points communicate with Raspberry Pi controllers, seamlessly identifying and logging each vehicle's entry and exit times.

Additionally, the system provides real-time monitoring of parking space availability, alleviating the frustration of searching for parking spots. Through a user-friendly interface accessible via web or mobile app, students, faculty, and visitors can easily locate vacant parking spaces within the campus, saving time and reducing congestion.

This project not only showcases the technical prowess of the students but also addresses a practical need within the college community. It embodies the spirit of innovation and collaboration fostered within the College of Engineering, promising to enhance the overall parking experience for all stakeholders.
